E-mail Writing

Electronic mail (email or e-mail) is a method of exchanging messages ("mail") between people using electronic devices. It is the easiest and the cheapest way of communication. It is used in formal, semi-formal as well as an informal way of expression or writing.



Categories

Emails are of three types:

  • Semi-Formal Email
  • Formal Email
  • Informal Email



Anatomy

Every email you write has the same basic structure: Subject line, greeting, email body, and closing. But as with every written form of professional communication, there’s a right way to do it and standards that should be followed. Here’s how to write a proper email:



Subject

Your subject line can entice people to open the message as well as set expectations about what’s enclosed.



Opener

You need to include a quick greeting to acknowledge the reader before diving into your main message or request.



Body

The body of an email is the meat of your message, and it must have a clear and specific purpose. It should also be concise.



Closings

This means writing a friendly sign-off.



Your Signature

Nothing else than your name and surname and possibly the job title.



Be Careful

  • Do not overuse long, complex sentences!
  • Do not overuse passive voice!
  • Avoid CapsLock! and capitalising everything
  • Do not use too informal language.
